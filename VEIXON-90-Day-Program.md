# VEIXON — The 90-Day Program (Day 1 → Day 90)
Every day is **one React route** — `/dashboard/warplan/[week]/[day]` — rendering the data below. This file is the readable view of `lib/constants/ninetyDayPlan.ts`. The 7-zone template renders it; VZN personalizes the texture per founder at runtime. Nobody hand-builds 90 pages.
**Rhythm inside every week:** Day1 *frame* → Days2-4 *execute* → Day5 *synthesize* → Day6 *apply* → Day7 *debrief*.
**Task modes:** `count` = +/- counter to a target · `competitiveContext` flags days that surface competitor weakness.
---

## PHASE 1 · VALIDATE (Days 1–28)

### Week 1 — OPERATION ZERO: Validate the problem
*Objective:* Confirm the problem is real and painful for a specific person  
*Day-7 milestone:* A one-sentence validated problem statement  
*Kill-switch:* 

**Day 1 · frame** — Name the exact problem and the exact person who has it.  
- Task (completion): Write a one-paragraph problem hypothesis that names a specific customer, not 'everyone'.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Who specifically feels this pain - name a real type of person? 2) When did you last watch this problem happen? 3) Why hasn't it already been solved well?  
- ✅ Success: A written hypothesis with one named, narrow customer segment.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Your customer is 'everyone' or 'businesses' - too vague to test.  
- Share card: "Day 1. Named the enemy. One problem, one person."

**Day 2 · execute** — Build a list of real people who match that customer.  
- Task (count (target 10) · ⚔️ competitor context): Find and list 10 real people who fit your target customer.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Where do these people already gather? 2) What proves someone actually fits the profile? 3) How will you reach each one?  
- ✅ Success: 10 named, reachable prospects in a list.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You can't find even 5 - the segment may not exist where you think.  
- Share card: "Day 2. Built my first 10-person target list."

**Day 3 · execute** — Open the conversations.  
- Task (count (target 10) · ⚔️ competitor context): Message 10 prospects and request a 15-minute problem interview.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's the one-line reason they'd say yes? 2) What channel gets the fastest reply? 3) What time window are you offering?  
- ✅ Success: 10 outreach messages sent, at least 3 replies.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Zero replies after 10 sends - message or channel is wrong.  
- Share card: "Day 3. Asked 10 strangers for their time."

**Day 4 · execute** — Listen, don't sell.  
- Task (count (target 4) · ⚔️ competitor context): Run at least 4 problem interviews - ask about their world, never pitch.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What will you NOT say (your idea)? 2) What's your opening question? 3) How will you capture exact quotes?  
- ✅ Success: 4 interviews done with real quotes captured.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You pitched instead of listening - the data is now biased.  
- Share card: "Day 4. 4 interviews. Zero pitching. Pure listening."

**Day 5 · synthesize** — Find the pattern in what you heard.  
- Task (completion): Write the 3 most common pain patterns from your interviews.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Which complaint came up the most? 2) What words did THEY use, not you? 3) What surprised you?  
- ✅ Success: 3 clearly written pain patterns grounded in quotes.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: No pattern repeats - too few interviews or no shared problem.  
- Share card: "Day 5. Found 3 patterns I didn't expect."

**Day 6 · apply** — Sharpen the problem into one sentence.  
- Task (completion): Rewrite your problem statement as one sentence a stranger would instantly recognize.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Can a stranger repeat it back? 2) Does it name the person AND the pain? 3) Have you cut every buzzword?  
- ✅ Success: One crisp sentence validated against a real quote.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Still needs a paragraph to explain - not sharp enough.  
- Share card: "Day 6. One sentence. The whole problem."

**Day 7 · debrief** — Go / no-go on the problem.  
- Task (completion): Decide: is this problem real and painful enough to continue? Document the evidence for the call.  
- Prep Qs: 1) How many people rated this a top-3 pain? 2) What's the strongest single piece of evidence? 3) What would make you walk away?  
- ✅ Success: A documented go/no-go backed by interview evidence.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You're continuing on hope, not evidence.  
- Share card: "Week 1 done. Problem validated with real evidence."

### Week 2 — OPERATION SIGNAL: Validate willingness to pay
*Objective:* Find out if anyone will actually exchange money for a fix  
*Day-7 milestone:* A documented 'yes' or a documented 'no, because X'  
*Kill-switch:* 

**Day 8 · frame** — Define what 'willing to pay' means here.  
- Task (completion): Write your price hypothesis: what they'd pay, how often, and what 'paying' looks like.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's the smallest real commitment that counts? 2) What price feels almost too high? 3) What are they paying for today instead?  
- ✅ Success: A concrete price + commitment definition.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: 'They'll definitely pay' with no number attached.  
- Share card: "Day 8. Put a number on it."

**Day 9 · execute** — Make the offer real.  
- Task (count (target 5) · ⚔️ competitor context): Present a paid offer or pre-order to at least 5 prospects and ask directly.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What exactly do they get for the money? 2) How do they say yes in one step? 3) What objection do you expect?  
- ✅ Success: 5 direct paid asks made.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You hinted at price but never actually asked.  
- Share card: "Day 9. Made my first real money ask."

**Day 10 · execute** — Push past the polite yes.  
- Task (count (target 5) · ⚔️ competitor context): Ask 5 more prospects and require a concrete next step (deposit, signature, calendar hold).  
- Prep Qs: 1) How do you separate polite interest from real intent? 2) What's the lowest-friction commitment? 3) Who's most likely to convert?  
- ✅ Success: 5 more asks with a concrete commitment requested.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Everyone 'loves it' but no one commits to anything.  
- Share card: "Day 10. Stopped accepting 'sounds great'."

**Day 11 · execute** — Chase the maybes.  
- Task (count · ⚔️ competitor context): Follow up with everyone who didn't say no and surface the real objection.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's the real reason behind 'let me think'? 2) Is it price, trust, or timing? 3) What would turn a maybe into a yes?  
- ✅ Success: Every open prospect followed up, objections logged.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You let the maybes go cold.  
- Share card: "Day 11. Hunted down every maybe."

**Day 12 · synthesize** — Tally the signal honestly.  
- Task (completion): List who said yes, who balked, and the exact reason for each.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's the dominant objection? 2) Did anyone pay or commit for real? 3) Which segment converts best?  
- ✅ Success: A clear yes/no tally with reasons.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You're rounding 'maybe' up to 'yes'.  
- Share card: "Day 12. Counted the real yeses."

**Day 13 · apply** — Fix the offer.  
- Task (completion): Revise the price or offer to answer the top objection, then re-ask 2 fence-sitters.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What single change kills the top objection? 2) Are you discounting or actually re-framing value? 3) Who gets the revised ask?  
- ✅ Success: A revised offer tested on real fence-sitters.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You dropped the price instead of fixing the value story.  
- Share card: "Day 13. Rebuilt the offer around the #1 objection."

**Day 14 · debrief** — Go / no-go on willingness to pay.  
- Task (completion): Document the verdict: is there real money signal here? Capture the proof either way.  
- Prep Qs: 1) How many credible commitments do you have? 2) What's the honest conversion rate of asks to yes? 3) What does the 'no' teach you?  
- ✅ Success: A documented willingness verdict with evidence.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Continuing despite zero real commitment.  
- Share card: "Week 2 done. Money signal: documented."

### Week 3 — OPERATION SKETCH: Design the smallest solution
*Objective:* Define the thinnest version of the product that tests the core value  
*Day-7 milestone:* A one-page spec of the MVP  
*Kill-switch:* 

**Day 15 · frame** — Define the single core value.  
- Task (completion): Write the one thing the MVP must do to deliver value - everything else is optional.  
- Prep Qs: 1) If it could only do ONE thing, what? 2) What's the moment the user feels value? 3) What are you tempted to add that you must not?  
- ✅ Success: One sentence naming the single core value.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Your 'core' is actually three features.  
- Share card: "Day 15. One job. That's the whole MVP."

**Day 16 · execute** — Map the critical path.  
- Task (completion): Sketch the exact step-by-step path a user takes from arrival to value moment.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's step one for a brand-new user? 2) Where could they get stuck? 3) What's the shortest path to value?  
- ✅ Success: A drawn user flow with no detours.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: The flow has 10 steps before any value.  
- Share card: "Day 16. Mapped the shortest path to value."

**Day 17 · execute** — Separate must-have from nice-to-have.  
- Task (completion): List every feature, then mark each must-have or cut for v1.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Does this feature block the core value? 2) What breaks if you remove it? 3) What can a human do manually instead of code?  
- ✅ Success: A ruthless must-have vs cut list.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Everything is 'must-have' - no real cuts made.  
- Share card: "Day 17. Cut half the feature list."

**Day 18 · execute** — Stress-test against a real user.  
- Task (count (target 2) · ⚔️ competitor context): Walk 2 prospects through the sketched flow and ask if it would actually solve their problem.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Does the flow match how THEY described the problem? 2) Where do they look confused? 3) What do they expect that's missing?  
- ✅ Success: 2 reactions to the sketch captured.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You only showed it to yourself.  
- Share card: "Day 18. Tested the sketch on real people."

**Day 19 · synthesize** — Write the one-page spec.  
- Task (completion): Write a single page: core value, user flow, must-have features, what's deliberately excluded.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Could an engineer build from this page alone? 2) Is the excluded list explicit? 3) Is it one page, not five?  
- ✅ Success: A complete one-page MVP spec.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: The spec sprawls past one page - scope crept back in.  
- Share card: "Day 19. Whole MVP on one page."

**Day 20 · apply** — Pick the build path.  
- Task (completion): Decide how you'll build it (no-code, code, or concierge/manual) and set the ship deadline.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's the fastest path to a real test? 2) Can you fake the backend manually first? 3) What's the hard deadline?  
- ✅ Success: A chosen build path with a dated deadline.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You picked the impressive path over the fast one.  
- Share card: "Day 20. Build path locked. Clock started."

**Day 21 · debrief** — Lock the scope.  
- Task (completion): Confirm the spec is final and buildable in under two weeks. Document the scope freeze.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Is every item buildable in your timeline? 2) What did you cut to get there? 3) What's the one feature you'll regret cutting - and why it's fine?  
- ✅ Success: A frozen, buildable one-page spec.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Scope still creeping - you'll never ship.  
- Share card: "Week 3 done. MVP scoped and frozen."

### Week 4 — OPERATION BUILD: Ship a working prototype
*Objective:* Get something real in front of a real user, even if ugly  
*Day-7 milestone:* A working link or demo, not a deck  
*Kill-switch:* 

**Day 22 · frame** — Plan the build sprint.  
- Task (completion): Break the MVP into the smallest shippable core path and schedule it across the week.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's the thinnest end-to-end slice? 2) What can you fake or stub for now? 3) What must work by Day 7?  
- ✅ Success: A day-by-day build plan for the core path.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You planned features, not a shippable slice.  
- Share card: "Day 22. Build sprint planned."

**Day 23 · execute** — Build the core path - part 1.  
- Task (completion): Build the first half of the critical path until a user can start the flow.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's the single most important screen/step? 2) What's blocking you right now? 3) What's the ugly-but-works version?  
- ✅ Success: The entry point of the flow works.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You're polishing pixels before the flow works.  
- Share card: "Day 23. First half of the flow is alive."

**Day 24 · execute** — Build the core path - part 2.  
- Task (completion): Build the second half so a user reaches the value moment end-to-end.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Does the value moment actually trigger? 2) What's still stubbed? 3) What's the fastest way to connect the ends?  
- ✅ Success: A user can go start-to-value, even if rough.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: The two halves still don't connect.  
- Share card: "Day 24. End-to-end path connected."

**Day 25 · execute** — Make it shareable.  
- Task (completion): Get the prototype to a link or demo you can put in someone else's hands.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Can a stranger open it without you? 2) What breaks on someone else's device? 3) What's the absolute minimum to share?  
- ✅ Success: A shareable working link or demo.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: It only works on your machine.  
- Share card: "Day 25. It's live. There's a link."

**Day 26 · synthesize** — Self-test and list the breaks.  
- Task (completion): Run the full flow yourself 3 times and list every breakage in priority order.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Where does a new user hit a wall? 2) Which break is most embarrassing? 3) Which break is fastest to fix?  
- ✅ Success: A prioritized list of breakages.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You tested the happy path only.  
- Share card: "Day 26. Found the cracks before users did."

**Day 27 · apply** — Fix the worst break.  
- Task (completion): Fix the top breakage so the core value reliably lands.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Does the core value now work every time? 2) Did the fix break anything else? 3) Is it good enough to demo live?  
- ✅ Success: The core path works reliably.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You fixed cosmetics, not the core break.  
- Share card: "Day 27. Killed the worst bug."

**Day 28 · debrief** — Demo it live.  
- Task (completion): Walk one real person through the working prototype live and document their reaction.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Did they reach the value moment unaided? 2) Where did they hesitate? 3) Would they use it again?  
- ✅ Success: A live demo done, reaction captured.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You described it instead of demoing it.  
- Share card: "Week 4 done. Working prototype in real hands."

## PHASE 2 · BUILD (Days 29–63)

### Week 5 — OPERATION FIRST FIVE: First real users
*Objective:* Get 5 people who are not friends or family actually using it  
*Day-7 milestone:* 5 logged users with at least one real session each  
*Kill-switch:* 

**Day 29 · frame** — Define a 'real session' and where to find five.  
- Task (completion · ⚔️ competitor context): Write what counts as a real session and list where 5 non-friends will come from.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What action proves a real session? 2) Which channel from Week 1 still has warm prospects? 3) Why would a stranger try it today?  
- ✅ Success: A session definition + a sourced list of 5.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: 'Real user' is undefined, so you'll fool yourself.  
- Share card: "Day 29. Defined what a real user means."

**Day 30 · execute** — Drive the first signups.  
- Task (count (target 2) · ⚔️ competitor context): Get the prototype in front of prospects and onboard the first 2 real users.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's the one-line reason to try it now? 2) What's the friction in signup? 3) Who's most likely to say yes today?  
- ✅ Success: 2 real users onboarded with a session each.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Only friends signed up.  
- Share card: "Day 30. First 2 real users in."

**Day 31 · execute** — Get to five.  
- Task (count (target 3) · ⚔️ competitor context): Onboard 3 more real users to reach five total with real sessions.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What worked for the first two? 2) Where are users dropping before value? 3) What's the fastest source of 3 more?  
- ✅ Success: 5 total real users with logged sessions.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Stuck at 2 - the source is dry.  
- Share card: "Day 31. Five real strangers using it."

**Day 32 · execute** — Watch them use it.  
- Task (count · ⚔️ competitor context): Observe or instrument what your users actually do and where they stop.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Where exactly do they drop off? 2) Do they reach the value moment? 3) What do they ignore entirely?  
- ✅ Success: Session behavior captured for all 5.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You're guessing at usage instead of measuring.  
- Share card: "Day 32. Watched real usage, not assumptions."

**Day 33 · synthesize** — Find the drop-off.  
- Task (completion): Identify the single biggest point where users stall or leave.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's the most common exit point? 2) Is it confusion, friction, or no value? 3) What did engaged users do differently?  
- ✅ Success: The #1 drop-off point, clearly named.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: No clear pattern - too few sessions to learn.  
- Share card: "Day 33. Found where users fall off."

**Day 34 · apply** — Fix the worst drop-off.  
- Task (completion): Make one change that reduces the biggest drop-off, then re-test with a user.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Does the change attack the real cause? 2) Can you test it with one user today? 3) What metric proves it worked?  
- ✅ Success: Drop-off fix shipped and re-tested.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You changed something unrelated to the drop-off.  
- Share card: "Day 34. Patched the leak."

**Day 35 · debrief** — Confirm five real sessions.  
- Task (completion): Verify you have 5 real users with genuine sessions and document the proof.  
- Prep Qs: 1) How many are truly non-friends? 2) Did each reach a real session? 3) Which user is most likely to return?  
- ✅ Success: 5 verified real sessions documented.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You're counting friends to hit the number.  
- Share card: "Week 5 done. Five real users, proven."

### Week 6 — OPERATION FEEDBACK: Cut what doesn't work
*Objective:* Talk to those users, find what they hate, and kill it  
*Day-7 milestone:* A revised feature list with at least one thing removed  
*Kill-switch:* 

**Day 36 · frame** — Write the feedback questions.  
- Task (completion): Write the 5 questions that reveal what users love, ignore, and hate.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What question reveals real value, not politeness? 2) How will you ask what they'd miss if it vanished? 3) How do you avoid leading them?  
- ✅ Success: 5 sharp, non-leading feedback questions.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Your questions beg for compliments.  
- Share card: "Day 36. Wrote questions that get the truth."

**Day 37 · execute** — Interview your users.  
- Task (count (target 4) · ⚔️ competitor context): Talk to at least 4 of your 5 users about what they actually use and avoid.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What do they open it for? 2) What have they never touched? 3) What frustrated them?  
- ✅ Success: 4 user feedback interviews done.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You asked 'do you like it?' and got 'yes'.  
- Share card: "Day 37. Heard the hard feedback."

**Day 38 · execute** — Dig into the ignored parts.  
- Task (count · ⚔️ competitor context): Probe specifically why the least-used features go untouched.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Is it undiscovered, confusing, or unwanted? 2) Would they notice if it disappeared? 3) What were they expecting instead?  
- ✅ Success: Clear reasons for each ignored feature.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You defended features instead of probing them.  
- Share card: "Day 38. Found what nobody uses."

**Day 39 · synthesize** — Name the loved and the dead.  
- Task (completion): Identify the one most-loved feature and the one most-ignored.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What's the single feature they'd fight to keep? 2) What's pure dead weight? 3) What surprised you about their priorities?  
- ✅ Success: One loved + one dead feature, named.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Everything seems equally 'fine' - dig deeper.  
- Share card: "Day 39. Found the one feature they love."

**Day 40 · apply** — Kill something.  
- Task (completion): Remove or hide the most-ignored feature and double down on the most-loved one.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What exactly are you removing? 2) How does cutting it sharpen the product? 3) How will you amplify the loved feature?  
- ✅ Success: One feature cut, the loved one strengthened.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You couldn't bring yourself to cut anything.  
- Share card: "Day 40. Killed a feature. On purpose."

**Day 41 · execute** — Ship the trimmed version.  
- Task (completion): Release the leaner product and tell users what changed.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Is the core value now more obvious? 2) Did removal break any flow? 3) How will you tell users?  
- ✅ Success: A leaner version shipped to users.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You trimmed it locally but never shipped.  
- Share card: "Day 41. Shipped a sharper, smaller product."

**Day 42 · debrief** — Confirm the cut was right.  
- Task (completion): Check whether users noticed the removal and document the revised feature list.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Did anyone miss the cut feature? 2) Is the value prop clearer now? 3) What's the next thing to cut?  
- ✅ Success: A documented, trimmed feature list.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You added features back under pressure.  
- Share card: "Week 6 done. Leaner product, proven cuts."

### Week 7 — OPERATION PROOF: First paying signal
*Objective:* Get a payment, a deposit, or a binding commitment to pay  
*Day-7 milestone:* First Rs.1 of real revenue or a signed pre-order  
*Kill-switch:* 

**Day 43 · frame** — Design the ask.

- ✅ Success: A concrete, one-step paid offer.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: The path to pay has 4 steps and no clear price.  
- Share card: "Day 43. Built a real way to pay."

**Day 44 · execute** — Ask your warmest users.  
- Task (count · ⚔️ competitor context): Make the paid ask to every engaged user and request a real commitment.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Who gets the most value today? 2) What's the natural moment to ask? 3) What objection do you expect from each?  
- ✅ Success: Every warm user asked to pay.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You hinted but never named the price.  
- Share card: "Day 44. Asked my best users for money."

**Day 45 · execute** — Widen the ask.  
- Task (count (target 5) · ⚔️ competitor context): Extend the paid offer to warm prospects beyond current users.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Which prospects already showed buy-signal? 2) What proof can you show them now? 3) What's the deadline that creates urgency?  
- ✅ Success: Paid offer extended to 5+ prospects.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You only asked people who'd already say no.  
- Share card: "Day 45. Took the offer wider."

**Day 46 · execute** — Handle the objections live.  
- Task (count · ⚔️ competitor context): Follow up on every 'maybe' and resolve the real blocker to payment.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Is it price, trust, or timing? 2) What proof closes the trust gap? 3) What's the smallest yes you'd accept?  
- ✅ Success: Every maybe followed up, blockers surfaced.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You let payment-ready people slip away.

**Day 47 · synthesize** — Analyze who paid and who didn't.  
- Task (completion): List converters vs non-converters and the deciding factor for each.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What do payers have in common? 2) What's the most common reason for no? 3) Which segment converts best?

- 🚩 Failure signal: You're averaging away the real signal.  
- Share card: "Day 47. Learned exactly who pays."

**Day 48 · apply** — Re-ask the fence-sitters.  
- Task (completion): Adjust the offer to the top objection and re-approach those who hesitated.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What single change unlocks the most yeses? 2) Are you fixing value or just cutting price? 3) Who's first to re-ask?  
- ✅ Success: Revised offer re-presented to hesitators.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You discounted to zero margin to force a yes.  
- Share card: "Day 48. Rebuilt the offer and re-asked."

**Day 49 · debrief** — Bank the proof.  
- Task (completion): Secure the first real payment or document precisely why it isn't happening yet.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Did real money or a binding commitment land? 2) If not, what's the exact blocker? 3) What's the path to first revenue?

### Week 8 — OPERATION MOAT: Start the defensibility
*Objective:* Identify and begin building what competitors can't copy in a sprint  
*Day-7 milestone:* A named, specific moat - not 'we move faster'  
*Kill-switch:* 

**Day 50 · frame** — List what's hard to copy.  
- Task (completion · ⚔️ competitor context): Write down every asset in your situation a competitor couldn't replicate in a week.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What do you have that took real time to build? 2) What relationship or data is uniquely yours? 3) What would a competitor struggle to copy?  
- ✅ Success: A list of candidate moats specific to you.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: Your list is all generic 'speed' and 'passion'.  
- Share card: "Day 50. Mapped what's actually hard to copy."

**Day 51 · execute** — Investigate the strongest candidate.  
- Task (count · ⚔️ competitor context): Research how durable your best moat candidate really is against competitors.  
- Prep Qs: 1) Could a funded competitor copy it in a month? 2) What makes it compound over time? 3) Who else is trying this?  
- ✅ Success: A reality-checked view of your top moat.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You assumed it's defensible without checking.  
- Share card: "Day 51. Pressure-tested my best moat."

**Day 52 · execute** — Gather the compounding asset.  
- Task (completion): Take concrete steps to accumulate the asset that strengthens with use (data, community, integrations).

- ✅ Success: First units of the compounding asset captured.  
- 🚩 Failure signal: You theorized a moat but built none of it.  
- Share card: "Day 52. Started stacking the moat."

**Day 53 · execute** — Talk to users about lock-in.  
- Task (count · ⚔️ competitor context): Ask users what would make leaving your product painful or pointless.  
- Prep Qs: 1) What would they lose by switching? 2) What keeps them coming back? 3) What would make them never leave?

**Day 54 · synthesize** — Name the one moat.  
- Task (completion): Choose the single moat you'll invest in and write why it's defensible.
